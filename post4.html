Who would you not love to visit Mount Pulag National Park?

Jennifer Pajalla-Bravo

Numerous plants and animals in Mount Pulag National Park are native to the mountain, exhibiting a great diversity. Its biodiversity includes endangered creatures including the Luzon Pygmy Fruit Bat, the Northern Luzon Giant Cloud Rat, and the Philippine Brown Deer. Other unusual flora like the pitcher plant may also be found, as well as a number of orchid species, some of which may be native to Mt. Pulag.

Mount Pulag “Playground of the Gods”

The peak of Mount Pulag is covered in grass and little bamboo trees. Lower on the mountain, a mossy woodland covered in fog and brimming with ferns, lichens, and moss may be found. The pine forest below is situated on steep, arid hills. The region is marked by falls, rivers, and little lakes.... The dwarf bamboo (Yushania niitakayamensis) and Benguet pine (Pinus kesiya), which predominate in sections of Luzon's tropical pine forests located on the mountainsides, are their native habitats. On Mount Pulag, you may find the Philippine yew tree, which has a chemical linked to cancer therapy. Native Ibaloi and Kalanguya populations prepare tea from its bark. With ferns, lichens, and moss in abundance, Mount Pulag features a mossy forest at its lower altitudes. Its natural biodiversity includes 33 bird species and numerous vulnerable animals, including the Philippine deer, the gigantic bushy-tailed cloud rat (bowet), and the long-haired fruit bat. The four cloud rat species can only be found in Mount Pulag. One of the most biodiverse areas in the Philippines, it is home to several endangered species, including the Koch pitta bird and the recently discovered (since 1896) 185-gram dwarf cloud rat.

Why do we travel Mount Pulag National Park?

Mount Pulag, the tallest peak in Luzon, is a popular destination for mountain climbers. The montane trees and the summit's grassland with its "sea of clouds" phenomena are the climb's highlights. The Ambangeg, Akiki, Tawangan, and the Ambangeo routes from Benguet, as well as the Ambaguio path from Nueva Vizcaya, are the four main trails that lead to the peak. The Department of Environment and Natural Resources' Mount Pulag National Park is in charge of overseeing these paths.

How to get to Mount Pulag National Park?

For those who are wishing to climb on weekends or during holidays, reservations for slots are often made on the first day of the preceding month (for March slots, make reservations on the first of February).
Note: Medical Certificate is a MUST. (You must be fit to climb the moment you decided to pursue climbing in Mt. Pulag. Depending on the 4 trails you will pick. Coordinate other fees upon reservation)
From Manila
• Ride a bus to going to Baguio --Victory Liner Bus Terminal in Cubao, EDSA 6-6.5 hours
From Baguio
• Ride a bus from Baguio to Kabayan or Ambangeg (earliest trip is 6:00 AM)
• From there, you can ride a habal-habal to the ranger station
• You can also hire a monster jeep that takes you from Baguio to the ranger station directly (PHP 7,000 – 9,000)
OR simply find a Team/Group you can be a Joiner, so you have nothing to worry about but pay for a certain amount.
