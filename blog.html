Mt. Pulag National Park -Benguet

The second tallest mountain in the Philippines and the highest point on Luzon is Mt. Pulag National Park, which rises to a height of 2,922 meters above the sea. The entire park is extremely rocky, with steep to extremely steep slopes at the mountain sides and generally rolling terrain at the mountain peak. It is part of the Philippine Cordillera Mountain Chain.

Mt. Daraitan Adventure Camp

In Tanay, Rizal, a neighborhood called Daraitan is tucked away among the Sierra Madre Mountain Range. In the middle of the mountain, there lies a calm and tranquil village. The 3,150 residents of the mountain barangay still maintain the characteristics of a traditional barrios: car-free streets, donkeys hauling cargo, roosters serving as the bariago's alarm clock.

The Islas De Gigantes – Carles, Iloilo

The name of a tree species native to the islands, Sabuluag or Salauag, was once used to refer to the Gigantes group. The name was changed to Gigantes at the time of Spanish colonization. According to a local tale, enormous sets of human bones were discovered within coffins inside Bakwitan Cave, which led to the name change. Residents also think engkantos live on the island.
