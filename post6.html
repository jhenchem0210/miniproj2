Who would you not love to visit The Islas De Gigantes –Carles, Iloilo?

The name of a tree species native to the islands, Sabuluag or Salauag, was once used to refer to the Gigantes group. The name was changed to Gigantes at the time of Spanish colonization. According to a local tale, enormous sets of human bones were discovered within coffins inside Bakwitan Cave, which led to the name change. Because of this, residents also think engkantos live on the island.

The Islas De Gigantes – Carles, Iloilo

The Islas De Gigantes are not nearly as busy as other well-known beach resorts in the Philippines since they are pretty far away—you must go there in less than two hours by local banca boat after driving there for two hours from Roxas City ... or three hours from Iloilo City. However, the lengthy journey to the Gigantes Islands is rewarded due to their breathtaking splendor. Without going on an island hopping excursion to explore its beautiful beaches and islands, a trip to Gigantes is not complete. At least a half-day is required to see the major attractions, which include Cabugao Dako Island, Bantigue Island, where the Bantigue sandbar is situated, Antonia Beach, Cabugao Gamay Island, Tangke Lagoon, Gigantes Norte Island, likewise Pawikan Cave.
One of the most visited beach destination in the country The Islas De Gigantes – Carles, Iloilo

Why do we travel The Islas De Gigantes?

Gigantes, informally referred to as the capital of scallops in the Philippines, offers affordable and fresh seafood. In addition, the islands are renowned for their peculiarly formed Wasay-wasay shells and profusion of fish, squid, crabs, and shrimp, among other aquatic delicacies. Seafood is more reasonably priced here and in Estancia than in the towns of Roxas and Iloilo, and it is unquestionably far less expensive than in Manila or Boracay. The steep granite cliffs along the Gigantes Sur coast conceal this little but lovely salt water lagoon. Take a swim in Tangke, then glance up at the sky to see if you can spot the star-shaped rock formations that completely encircle the serene pool's green hue. Tangke is most beautiful at high tide, so be sure you go.

How to get to The Isla De Gigantes?

Fly to Roxas City, Capiz Province, from Ninoy Aquino International Airport. Then travel by land from Roxas City to Islas de Gigantes which might take up to 5 hours and 30 minutes.
