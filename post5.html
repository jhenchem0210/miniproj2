Who would you not love to visit Mt. Daraitan Adventure Camp?

JANUARY 22, 2023 -— Views: 100

Written by

Jennifer Pajalla-Bravo

In Tanay, Rizal, a neighborhood called Daraitan is tucked away among the Sierra Madre Mountain Range. In the middle of the mountain, there lies a calm and tranquil village. The 3,150 residents of the mountain barangay still maintain the characteristics of a traditional barrios: car-free streets, donkeys hauling cargo, roosters serving as the bariago's alarm clock, wells and springs providing the community with drinking water, and organic farmland where the majority of the fresh produce is sourced. Tourists are always greeted with a grin by the locals. They are kind, welcoming, and considerate.

Batlag Falls | Mt. Daraitan

You may pass through several locations en route to the Daraitan River, including Kalinawan Cave, Daranak Falls, and Batlag Falls. The Balete Cave, so named because of the Balete tree that stands at its entrance, is another cave close to the Daraitan River.... One of the well-known mountains in Tanay, Rizal's premier municipality, is Mount Daraitan. The area has the best that nature has to offer, from clear rivers and lovely caves—an estimated 100-year-old structure made of pillar and pebble rocks—to a great view of the Sierra Madre mountain range and the lovely Tinipak River. Thousands of hikers, tourists, and adventure seekers flock to the lovely hiking paths of Mt. Daraitan each week, quickly establishing itself as one of the most popular hiking destinations in the Philippines.
One of the most visited park in the country MT. DARAITAN AND TINIPAK RIVER | TANAY, RIZAL

Why do we travel Mt. Daraitan Adventure Camp?

I've witnessed this type of free-flowing river and am undoubtedly engrossed in the beauty of nature. As we walked ahead, we noticed many people bathing in the clean river. The water is also crystal pure and encircled by large marble pebbles. Awe-inspiring to see with my own eyes are the clear waters of Tinipak River, gorgeous white limestone rocks, and mountain cliffs that surround it. We take a little break outside the Tinipak Cave after our hall of fame stroll to the Tinipak River. It is rated as being 4 out of 9 challenging. We had a hard time bouldering upward. We have the impression that there is no wind at all within the mountain. The trip to the campground takes roughly 4-5 hours, with an additional 20 minutes for the assault march to the peak. We set up camp for the night, and just before daybreak we began our ascent. A breath-taking reward awaits you after the strenuous journey to the peak. The Tinipak River is home to rushing torrents, magnificent rock formations, and a cave so stunning that it has a built-in natural bathtub.

How to get to Mt. Daraitan?

Via Public Transportation
To go to Tanay, Rizal, take a van or a jeep in front of EDSA-Shaw Boulevard or in front of Starmall. Take a tricycle to the Barangay Daraitan registration location from Jollibee Tanay. The tricycle journey takes 30 to 40 minutes to complete. The cost is 500 pesos for a tricycle or 100 pesos per person. Another choice is to take a jeep from Aurora Boulevard in Cubao to Antipolo Cogeo, get out at the public market in Antipolo Cogeo, then take another jeep to Sampaloc in Tanay, and then take a tricycle to the Barangay Daraitan registration location.
Using a private car
Take the Tanay-Infanta Road to Marcos Highway and continue on it. Turn left into the rocky road that goes to Barangay Daraitan, continue until you see the river crossing and registration hall, which is a few meters past the river, and then park your car there. It takes two to five hours to reach there.
